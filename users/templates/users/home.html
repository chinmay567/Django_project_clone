The main difference between a homepage and a landing page is that the homepage has dozens of possible distractions. In contrast, the landing pages are extremely focused on conveying a specific action, and directing users to a particular direction. It is a well-known fact by now that having fewer links on your landing page increases conversions, as there are fewer alluring clickables that’ll distract visitors away from your call to action. Sure, the homepage is the one showing off the brand, letting people explore a range of products, and also offering additional info about the company and its values. From here, a visitor can go anywhere – apply for a job, read some press releases, review the terms of service, post on the community boards, et cetera. Therefore, they won’t necessarily have to make a purchase. And so, here is where the landing page comes in handy. Paired with smart ads that promote a particular and single offer with a specific message, everything about it works hard to turn mere visitors into customers.
